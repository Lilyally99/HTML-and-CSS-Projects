Cardiomyopathies

Cardiomyopathies are diseases of the heart muscle that can affect structure and function. This page summarizes common types, key features, and includes images and videos for learning.

Types of Cardiomyopathies

Dilated Cardiomyopathy (DCM)

Characterized by left ventricular dilation and reduced systolic function (often EF is less than 35%). Causes include genetic disease, viral myocarditis, alcohol, and peripartum cardiomyopathy.

Video: DCM / Cardiomyopathy Overview

Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)

Thickening of the heart muscle, often genetic. Features include LV hypertrophy (commonly asymmetric septal hypertrophy) and may cause LVOT obstruction, diastolic dysfunction, and arrhythmias.

Video: Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy

Restrictive Cardiomyopathy (RCM)

Reduced ventricular compliance leading to impaired relaxation. Causes can include infiltration or fibrosis. Examples include cardiac amyloidosis and sarcoidosis.
